What is an NGO?

A Non-Governmental Organization (NGO) is a non-profit group that works independently of government, usually to address a social or political issue. NGOs often focus on education, healthcare, environment, child welfare, and more. They serve as bridges between people in need and the resources or knowledge required to uplift them.

How Do NGOs Operate?

NGOs operate through the support of volunteers, donations, corporate partnerships, and international grants. Their work includes running schools, organizing health camps, providing relief in disasters, empowering youth and women, and creating awareness about important issues. Transparency, trust, and impact are essential to their work.

Case Study: Basti Ki Pathshala

Basti Ki Pathshala is a powerful example of grassroots education. Run by student volunteers in Indian slums, it provides free classes to children who cannot afford formal schooling. Classes are held in open areas, using donated supplies and a lot of heart. Learn more about this inspiring initiative at bastikipathshala.org.
